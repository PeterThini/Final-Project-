Background

Prioritizing Agriculture in Kenya: A Path to Sustainable Development


Agriculture is the backbone of Kenya's economy, contributing approximately 30% of the GDP and employing nearly 40% of the total population. In the 2024 Global Hunger Index, Kenya ranks 100th out of the 127 countries with sufficient data to calculate 2024 GHI scores. With a score of 25.0 in the 2024 Global Hunger Index, Kenya has a level of hunger that is serious.

However, challenges such as erratic weather patterns, declining soil fertility, lack of acess to affordable and quality fertilizer and limited access to modern farming technology threaten its potential. Prioritizing agriculture is critical to ensure food security, drive economic growth, and achieve sustainable development. Smart agriculture offers a transformative solution by leveraging technology and data to optimize farming practices. Tools such as precision farming and weather forecasting can help farmers adapt to climate change by selecting crops suitable for shifting weather patterns. Access to certified, drought-resistant, and high-yield seed varieties ensures productivity, even in unpredictable conditions. Investment in modern irrigation systems and agroforestry can further enhance water efficiency and environmental sustainability.

By prioritizing agriculture, Kenya addresses multiple Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), including:
✓ SDG 1 (No Poverty) by creating income generating opportunities.
✓ SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) through improved food production and security.
✓ SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth) by bolstering the agro-economy.
✓ SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production) via sustainable farming practices and recycling.
✓ SDG 13 (Climate Action) by promoting climate smart agricultural strategies.
